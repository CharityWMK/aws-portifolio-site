About Me

I'm a passionate ICT professional who has spent over nine years at the heart of enterprise technology, keeping hospitals secure, fintech platforms running, and teams performing at their best.

I started my career at Metropolitan Hospital Nairobi, where I grew from setting up computers and fixing printers all the way to becoming the hospital's Information Security Officer. That journey taught me how IT really works from the ground up — the cables, the servers, the firewalls, the people, and the pressure of keeping mission-critical systems alive.

From there I took my skills into fintech at 4G Capital, where I led a team of seven IT technicians, built out the organisation's IT infrastructure, and managed everything from Google Workspace to disaster recovery planning. Now I'm at Medtronic LABS, contributing to technology that improves healthcare access across Africa.

Throughout it all, I've been driven by one belief: that technology, when it works well, quietly changes lives. And I want to build more of that at scale, in the cloud.

Metropolitan Hospital Nairobi · Healthcare
Metropolitan Hospital Nairobi Jun 2017 — Apr 2023 · ~6 yrs Healthcare

Metropolitan Hospital is where I really grew up in tech. What started as a fresh-from-university attachment turned into nearly six years of increasingly complex and critical work — and I wore many hats over that time.

In the early days I was the person on the ground: installing the hospital's HMIS, repairing hardware, configuring LAN systems, and providing frontline support to hundreds of clinical and administrative staff. I also integrated imaging and diagnostic equipment with the PACS server — a technically demanding project that sharpened my precision.

Growing into System Administrator, I configured a high-availability data recovery site, managed daily database clustering and backups, and monitored the entire environment using Zabbix. I administered Microsoft 365 for all staff and supported critical microservices: M-Pesa C2B, Bulk SMS, lab systems, eClaims, and more.

Ultimately I was trusted with the hospital's most sensitive responsibility: Information Security Officer. I deployed AlienVault OSSIM SIEM for real-time threat detection, configured Kaspersky Endpoint Security Cloud, managed Sophos firewall policies, and ran cybersecurity awareness training for the entire hospital.
